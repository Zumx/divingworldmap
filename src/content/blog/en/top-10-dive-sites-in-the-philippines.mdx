The Philippines sits at the centre of the Coral Triangle, the most biodiverse marine region on the planet, and the statistics speak for themselves: more than 500 species of hard coral, over 2,000 fish species, and a roster of dive sites that range from world-class current walls to bomb-blasted muck slopes being lovingly restored. The archipelago of more than 7,600 islands means that conditions vary enormously by region and season. The Visayas are generally best from November to May, Palawan from October through May, and the north around Batangas year-round. Water temperatures stay between 26 °C and 30 °C throughout the country, making a 3 mm wetsuit sufficient almost everywhere. What makes the Philippines unique among the world's premier dive destinations is the breadth of diving style available within a relatively compact geography. A diver can spend their morning on a thresher-shark seamount, their afternoon photographing pygmy seahorses in the shallows, and their evening descending into the hold of a WWII wreck — and do all three within a short banca ride of a single island. The infrastructure is correspondingly diverse: modest bamboo guesthouses running two dives a day from a dugout, boutique resorts with underwater photography workshops, and serious liveaboards capable of reaching the most remote atolls in the Sulu Sea.

## 1. Tubbataha Reef, Palawan

Tubbataha is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and arguably the Philippines' most spectacular atoll system, accessible only by liveaboard and only during the brief season between March and June when the Sulu Sea calms enough for passage. Two atolls 33 kilometres apart plunge from the surface to several hundred metres on all sides. Hammerhead and grey reef sharks patrol the outer walls, while manta rays and leopard sharks use the shallower lagoons. The nutrient-rich upwellings mean visibility typically exceeds 30 metres, and the coral coverage on the upper walls is among the finest remaining in Asia.

## 2. Apo Reef, Mindoro

Apo Reef National Park is the second-largest contiguous coral reef in the world and sits off the coast of Mindoro Occidental. It lacks the logistical drama of Tubbataha — it is reachable by banca from Sablayan in about two hours — but it delivers comparable biomass. Schools of bumphead parrotfish the size of boulders charge across the shallow reef crest at dawn, and white-tip reef sharks rest in channels throughout the day. Maximum depths on the outer reef exceed 40 metres. The best conditions run from October through May.

## 3. Malapascua, Cebu

Malapascua is the only place in the world where thresher sharks — the species with the extraordinary elongated tail — are reliably sighted on cleaning stations. Monad Shoal, a submerged seamount sitting at 25–30 metres, hosts these daily cleaning visits, typically between dawn and about eight in the morning. Beyond the thresher encounters, Malapascua's macro sites such as Lighthouse and Gato Island are outstanding, with mandarin fish performing their mating ritual each evening on the rubble fields. Advanced Open Water recommended for Monad Shoal.

## 4. Coron, Palawan

Coron Bay holds one of the greatest wreck diving collections in Asia: a fleet of Japanese supply ships sunk by American aircraft on 24 September 1944 now rests at depths between 15 and 40 metres. The Okikawa Maru, Irako, and Kogyo Maru are the most visited, each large enough to spend two dives exploring without covering the same ground twice. Soft coral and sponge growth has transformed the hulls into genuine artificial reefs, and schools of jackfish shelter in the engine rooms. PADI Advanced Open Water certification is typically required by local operators for the deeper wrecks.

## 5. Anilao, Batangas

Anilao is one of the most accessible dive destinations in the Philippines — three hours from Manila — and its reputation as a macro photography destination is unsurpassed. The volcanic rubble and mixed reef of sites like Twin Rocks, Cathedral Rock and Sombrero Island host mimic octopus, frogfish in multiple colour morphs, nudibranchs in the hundreds, and hairy ghost pipefish. Visibility is moderate — typically 8 to 15 metres — and current is manageable, making it suitable from Open Water level upward. The site is diveable year-round.

## 6. Twin Reef

Twin Reef is one of the signature sites within the Anilao complex, formed by two parallel ridges whose sides are smothered in soft coral. The cavities between the ridges funnel current and concentrate schooling fish — fusiliers, anthias, and batfish — making it as effective for wide-angle shooting as for macro work. Maximum depth runs to about 28 metres on the seaward side of the outer ridge.

## 7. Fantastic Reef

Fantastic Reef, also within the Anilao area, earns its name from coral coverage that rivals anything in the Coral Triangle. Gorgonian sea fans up to two metres across grow at around 20 metres, and the shallow sections are carpeted with table corals that shelter juvenile reef fish. A PADI Open Water certification is adequate for the upper reef profile; going deeper toward the sandy slope at 30 metres warrants Advanced certification.

## 8. Rockport Dive Site

Rockport is a shore dive on the Batangas coast that has become popular with photographers seeking consistent muck-diving conditions without the travel cost of Lembeh or Dumaguete. The black-sand slope begins at about 5 metres and extends to 25, and regular night dives here reveal ornate ghost pipefish, various shrimp species, and the occasional ribbon eel transitioning between colour phases.

## 9. Sampaguita

Sampaguita is a reef site in the Sibuyan Sea with a vertical drop-off that begins at the surface and falls beyond recreational limits. The sheer wall is colonised by wire coral, crinoids and large barrel sponges. Pelagic visitors include white-tip and silver-tip sharks, and on clear days visibility can extend to 25 metres. Best dived at slack water as the current can be significant when the tide runs. Advanced certification recommended.

## 10. Coral Garden, Batangas

Coral Garden is a deliberately accessible site — suitable for Open Water divers and frequently used for check dives by dive centres in the Puerto Galera area. The reef slopes gently from 5 to 18 metres and is among the better-preserved shallow reefs on the Batangas coast. Turtles are resident, and the density of small reef fish makes it excellent for teaching navigation and buoyancy. It represents what the Philippine reef system looks like when anchor damage and blast fishing are absent.

## Practical information

Domestic flights connect Manila with Cebu, Coron and Puerto Princesa (for Palawan). Banca (outrigger) boat is the standard vessel for day diving throughout the archipelago. Many remote sites require overnight island stays or liveaboard access. PADI and SSI are the dominant certification agencies; courses are available at almost every resort.
