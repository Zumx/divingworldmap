Mozambique's coastline extends nearly 2,500 kilometres from Tanzania in the north to South Africa in the south, much of it fringed by reefs that have remained comparatively undisturbed by dive traffic. The country entered the international diving consciousness primarily through two sites: Tofo, on the central coast, which offers some of the world's most reliable whale shark and manta ray encounters, and the Bazaruto Archipelago, a protected marine park with exceptional coral health. Beyond these flagships, the Mozambique Channel hosts a productivity driven by the Agulhas Current system that brings large marine animals north with remarkable regularity. Water temperature rarely drops below 23°C and peaks at 29°C in summer. Visibility varies by site and season but regularly exceeds 20 metres on the outer reefs.

## 1. Manta Reef, Tofo

Manta Reef — the specific site within the broader Tofo area — is a pinnacle and reef system at eight to 25 metres where reef manta rays gather at cleaning stations with a consistency that has made it one of the most-studied manta populations in the Indian Ocean. The Manta Trust has catalogued hundreds of individual rays here over years of dedicated research. Dives involve descending to a cleaning station at 15 to 20 metres, positioning on the reef, and waiting for mantas to arrive — sometimes within minutes, sometimes requiring patience, but sightings rates run above 80% for most of the year. The pinnacles themselves are excellent coral structures with diverse reef fish. Open Water level.

## 2. Whale Shark Aggregation, Tofo Beach

The open water off Tofo's beach, including a section of reef called Manta Point, regularly concentrates whale sharks from October through March when upwellings bring the plankton on which they feed. These are some of the most reliable whale shark encounters in the Indian Ocean — not a snorkelling-only experience but a genuine scuba dive alongside animals reaching 10 to 12 metres in length. Depths during encounters vary as the sharks move through the water column; encounters commonly occur between five and 20 metres. The Tofo operators who run these dives have years of experience reading current and surface signs to locate aggregations. Advanced Open Water recommended to manage the variable conditions.

## 3. Bazaruto Archipelago Outer Reef

The Bazaruto Archipelago, a protected national park north of Inhambane, hosts some of the cleanest coral reef in Mozambique. The outer reef faces of Bazaruto and Benguerra Islands drop from the surface to 30 metres and beyond with strong hard coral coverage — Acropora formations in excellent condition, sea fans on the deeper walls, and a fish community including dugong (the archipelago holds one of the last viable East African dugong populations), sea turtles, dolphins, and large pelagics on the outer reef. Access requires a boat from Vilanculos. The park entry fee funds genuine conservation. Best dived June through November; the cyclone season limits access from December through March.

## 4. Two Mile Reef, Ponta do Ouro

Ponta do Ouro at the southern tip of Mozambique, adjacent to the South African border, is home to Two Mile Reef — a long shallow reef complex at three to 20 metres that is the gathering point for an extraordinary daily parade of bottlenose dolphins. The dolphins are habituated to divers and approach closely; it is common to share the water with pods of 20 to 50 animals for the full dive. Beyond the dolphins, the reef hosts blacktip and bull sharks (the latter in the deeper channels), eagle rays, sea turtles, and dense fish populations. Open Water level for the reef top. Bull shark dives deeper in the channel require Advanced certification and operator briefing.

## 5. Pinnacles, Tofo

A series of submerged pinnacles off Tofo, descending from 15 to 35 metres, provides an alternative to the cleaning-station dives when manta activity is low. The pinnacles function as aggregation points for large pelagics: schools of trevally, barracuda, and kingfish use the current breaks, while the pinnacle walls carry good soft coral growth and a dense fish community of snapper, grouper, and surgeonfish. Occasional tiger shark passes through at the base. Advanced Open Water for the deeper pinnacles; the top sections are accessible from Open Water level. Best at the turn of the tide when current pauses and visibility peaks.

## 6. Dreamhouse Reef, Tofo

Dreamhouse is a recreational reef dive for less experienced divers or as a second dive after a deep pinnacle or manta session. The reef system sits at five to 18 metres with good hard coral coverage and reliable turtle encounters. The shallower sections suit photographers working macro — nudibranchs, flatworms, and small crustaceans are found in the coral rubble. The site's relatively sheltered position means visibility is consistent at 15 to 20 metres for most of the year. A good site for refresher divers reacquainting themselves with the ocean and for divers on their first multi-day trip to Mozambique. Open Water level.

## 7. Giraul Point, Bazaruto

On the northern side of the Bazaruto Archipelago, Giraul Point marks the edge of the outer barrier where the reef wall drops steeply to beyond 40 metres. The wall is characterised by large sea fans and black coral colonies at depth, alongside passing rays, sharks, and occasionally the larger pelagics that sweep through on the Mozambique Channel currents. Inside the wall, the lagoon reef at five to 15 metres is covered in healthy hard coral and hosts a juvenile fish nursery that makes it excellent for macro photography. Advanced Open Water for the wall; Open Water for the inner reef. Best from July through October.

## 8. Manta Bay, Inhambane

North of Tofo in the broad sweep of Inhambane Bay, Manta Bay is a cleaning station site shallower and calmer than Manta Reef — depths of six to 15 metres over a patchy reef and sandy bottom. The cleaning stations here are used primarily by reef mantas but also occasionally by oceanic mantas that move inshore on the bay's productivity. The calmer conditions make this site appropriate for less experienced divers and for those who found the conditions at Manta Reef challenging. Night dives at Manta Bay have produced encounters with leopard sharks resting on the sand. Open Water level.

## 9. Coco Reef, Ponta do Ouro

A structured coral reef at three to 15 metres off Ponta do Ouro, Coco Reef provides an accessible dive with the complementary attraction of resident ragged-tooth sharks (Carcharias taurus) in the summer months from November through March. Raggies aggregate here to mate and are docile in the presence of experienced divers who don't crowd them. The reef community itself includes moray eels, large pufferfish, and cleaning stations busy with activity. The site is appropriate for Open Water divers who have been briefed on shark behaviour and protocol. Ponta do Ouro's operators take this briefing seriously and the encounters are well-managed.

## 10. Santa Maria Reef, Inhambane Peninsula

On the long sandy peninsula that forms the western arm of Inhambane Bay, Santa Maria Reef is a fringing system at three to 15 metres that gives area-based divers an alternative to the boat trips to Tofo. The reef is less spectacular than the outer sites but supports a healthy population of sea turtles, rays, and reef fish that makes it a valid afternoon or discovery dive. Several small operators in Inhambane town run trips to Santa Maria. The site's protection from oceanic swell means conditions are predictable even when ocean weather limits access to the outer sites. A practical and pleasant option for divers based in Inhambane.

## Planning Your Mozambique Dive Trip

The diving calendar in Mozambique splits neatly by activity. For whale sharks and manta rays at Tofo, October through January is prime — water temperature is 26–28°C and upwellings concentrate the plankton. For Bazaruto's coral and general reef diving, June through November gives the best visibility and settled seas. The cyclone season from December through March affects the archipelago significantly; Tofo is more sheltered. Most visitors fly into Vilanculos for Bazaruto or Inhambane for Tofo. Road from Maputo to Tofo is manageable by 4WD. Operators in Tofo are primarily PADI-affiliated with genuine wildlife expertise built from years of data collection. Hyperbaric facilities are limited to Maputo — factor this into your dive planning and err conservative.
