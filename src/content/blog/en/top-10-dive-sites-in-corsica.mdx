Corsica rises from the Mediterranean as a mountain dropped into the sea, and its diving carries the same granite drama below the waterline. The island's coast alternates between the red porphyry of the west, the granite of the south and the marine reserves that have made its waters some of the richest in the Mediterranean. The Lavezzi archipelago between Corsica and Sardinia, the Scandola reserve on the west coast and the bay of Calvi all protect dense fish life and dramatic underwater terrain. Add the famous B-17 bomber wreck off Calvi, water reaching 25°C in late summer, and visibility that frequently passes 30 metres, and Corsica earns its place among the Mediterranean's finest dive regions.

## 1. The B-17 Bomber, Calvi

The B-17 Flying Fortress in the bay of Calvi is one of the most celebrated wreck dives in the Mediterranean. The American bomber ditched in the bay in February 1944 after engine trouble; the crew escaped, and the aircraft settled almost intact onto white sand at around 28 metres. The four engines, the wings and the fuselage are clearly recognisable, colonised now by groupers and shoaling fish. The depth and the importance of preserving the wreck make it an Advanced dive run by Calvi operators. Visibility is excellent over the pale sand, often 30 metres. Best from May to October.

## 2. Mérouville, Lavezzi Islands

Off the granite Lavezzi archipelago at Corsica's southern tip near Bonifacio, the site nicknamed Mérouville — Grouper Town — lives up to its name. Inside the protected Bouches de Bonifacio nature reserve, dozens of enormous dusky groupers have grown so tame they approach divers at close range over granite boulders at 12 to 25 metres. The reserve, shared with Sardinia's Maddalena across the strait, protects an exceptional density of fish. The site suits Open Water divers in calm conditions. Visibility frequently exceeds 30 metres. Dived from Bonifacio, best from June to September.

## 3. La Vacca and the Lavezzi Reefs

The granite reefs around the Lavezzi islands offer some of the Mediterranean's most beautiful clear-water diving. La Vacca and the surrounding boulder fields drop from 6 to 30 metres through water of extraordinary clarity, the rounded granite sculpted into arches, gullies and swim-throughs. Groupers, dentex, barracuda and octopus inhabit the rock, with eagle rays over the sand. The protected reserve keeps the fish abundant. Sites suit Open Water through Advanced divers. Visibility is among the best in the Mediterranean, often 35 metres. The season runs May through October.

## 4. Scandola Marine Reserve, Western Corsica

The Scandola reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage site on Corsica's wild west coast near Girolata, protects red porphyry cliffs above water and dramatic reef below. Diving access is controlled to preserve the ecosystem, and the reward is some of Corsica's richest marine life — groupers, barracuda, amberjack and dense shoals over reef from 10 to 35 metres. The red volcanic rock gives the underwater landscape a striking colour. Sites suit Open Water through Advanced divers. Visibility frequently passes 30 metres. Dived from Porto and Galéria, best in the calm summer months.

## 5. Punta Rossa, Bonifacio

Beneath the famous white limestone cliffs of Bonifacio, Punta Rossa offers dramatic wall diving where the rock plunges from the surface past 40 metres. Groupers, dentex and barracuda patrol the wall, while lobster and moray eels shelter in the crevices and red gorgonians fan from the deeper rock. The site combines spectacular topography with the rich fish life of the southern Corsican reserves. Depth makes it an Advanced dive. Visibility is typically 25 to 30 metres. Boats run from Bonifacio harbour, with the best conditions from June to September.

## 6. Tonnara and the Reefs of Porto-Vecchio

Off the granite coast near Porto-Vecchio in south-east Corsica, the reefs and seamounts at Tonnara and the surrounding waters offer accessible diving over boulder fields and posidonia from 8 to 30 metres. Groupers, octopus, scorpionfish and shoals of bream populate the rock, and the sheltered bays make for calm, beginner-friendly conditions. The granite sculpting and clear water are typical of southern Corsica. Sites suit Open Water divers and relaxed second dives. Visibility runs 20 to 30 metres. The diving season runs comfortably from May to October.

## 7. Danger de l'Algajola, Balagne

Off the Balagne coast between Calvi and Île-Rousse, the Danger de l'Algajola is a rocky reef rising near the surface and dropping past 30 metres, washed by current that brings in dentex, barracuda and amberjack. Groupers and large shoals of bream inhabit the granite, and the deeper rock holds red gorgonians. The reef suits Advanced divers comfortable with depth and mild current. Visibility is typically 20 to 30 metres. Boats run from Calvi and Île-Rousse, with the warmest, calmest water from July to September.

## 8. Capo Rosso, Western Corsica

Near Piana on the west coast, the dramatic red cliffs of Capo Rosso continue underwater as walls and reefs of red porphyry descending past 35 metres. The striking rock colour, combined with groupers, barracuda and dense shoals, makes this one of Corsica's most photogenic dives. The nearby Calanques de Piana are a UNESCO site, and the marine landscape matches the drama above water. Depth makes the walls an Advanced dive; shallower reefs welcome Open Water divers. Visibility frequently exceeds 30 metres. Dived from Porto, best in summer.

## 9. The Reefs of Cap Corse

The long northern peninsula of Cap Corse offers wilder, less-dived sites over granite reef and the occasional wreck. Walls and boulder fields run from 10 to 35 metres in clear water, with groupers, dentex, barracuda and the chance of pelagic fish on the current-washed points. The remoteness keeps the sites uncrowded and the fish life rich. Conditions suit Open Water through Advanced divers, with some exposure to wind on the open points. Visibility is typically 20 to 30 metres. The season runs May to October, calmest in late summer.

## 10. Île de Cavallo and the Southern Granite

Between the Lavezzi and the mainland, the granite islet of Cavallo and its surrounding reefs offer clear-water diving over sculpted boulders from 8 to 28 metres. Inside the Bonifacio reserve, the fish life is exceptional — groupers, dentex, barracuda and octopus over rounded granite in water of remarkable clarity. The sheltered position between islands keeps conditions calm. Sites suit Open Water through Advanced divers. Visibility is among the finest in the Mediterranean, often 30 metres or more. Dived from Bonifacio, best from June through September.

## Explore on the map

Corsica rewards matching the coast to the diving: the south around Bonifacio and the Lavezzi for clear-water granite and the famous Mérouville groupers; the west at Scandola and Capo Rosso for red porphyry walls and protected reserves; and the Balagne around Calvi for the B-17 bomber and offshore reefs. The season runs roughly May to October, warmest in August.
