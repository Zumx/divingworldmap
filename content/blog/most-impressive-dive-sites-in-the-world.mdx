Some dives are not just good — they are the experiences that define the sport,
through biodiversity, big animals, or a spectacle the ocean stages only here.
These are diving's icons.

## Raja Ampat, Indonesia

The most biodiverse marine region on Earth — the absolute peak of reef and fish
density anywhere.

## Galápagos, Ecuador

Schooling hammerheads, whale sharks, and marine iguanas — wild, cold,
current-driven, and unforgettable (advanced).

## Cocos Island, Costa Rica

A liveaboard-only seamount famous for vast hammerhead schools — "the most
beautiful island in the world" underwater.

## Sardine Run, South Africa

A moving feast — bait balls hunted by sharks, dolphins, and whales along the
Wild Coast.

## Great Blue Hole, Belize

A perfect submarine sinkhole — a diving legend more for the descent than the
life.

## Barracuda Point, Sipadan, Malaysia

Tornadoes of barracuda and jackfish off an oceanic island wall.

## SS Thistlegorm, Egypt

The greatest wreck dive in the world — a WWII cargo ship frozen with its load
in the Red Sea.

## Silfra, Iceland

Diving between two continental plates in glacial water of impossible clarity.

## Komodo, Indonesia

Manta-filled, current-charged reefs beneath an island of dragons.

## Palau

Blue Corner's sharks and Jellyfish Lake — a marine sanctuary nation's
masterpiece.

## What makes a dive impressive

Biodiversity overwhelms (Raja Ampat), big animals thrill (Galápagos, Cocos, the
Sardine Run), and singular spectacle astonishes (Silfra's clarity, the
Thistlegorm's cargo). The greatest dives combine the wonder with genuine
wilderness — and usually demand real skill.

## Diving the icons responsibly

Most of these are advanced: strong currents, depth, remoteness, liveaboard-only
access, and minimum-experience requirements. Build up to them honestly, dive
with conservation-led operators, hold the required certifications, maintain
perfect buoyancy and no-contact discipline, and follow every marine-park rule.
These ecosystems are world treasures under pressure.
