Germany has a huge, skilled diving community built on clear quarry lakes,
alpine waters, and the Baltic — proof that world-class training and fun
diving do not require the tropics. These ten stand out.

## 1. Walchensee, Bavaria

A deep, clear alpine lake — Germany's premier mountain dive, cold and
spectacular.

## 2. Kreidesee Hemmoor

A flooded chalk quarry with astonishing visibility and submerged machinery —
arguably Germany's most famous inland site.

## 3. Sundhäuser See, Thuringia

A clear lake with sunken attractions, a training and fun-dive favourite.

## 4. Fernsteinsee region / Blautopf-style springs

Karst spring pools of vivid blue clarity beloved by technical divers.

## 5. Baltic Sea wrecks (Fehmarn, Rügen)

Cold-water wreck diving with well-preserved hulls in the brackish Baltic.

## 6. Helene quarry, Ruhr

A popular, well-organised training lake with platforms and attractions.

## 7. Attersee (nearby, alpine type)

Representative of the cold, clear alpine-lake diving German divers travel for.

## 8. Löbejün quarry, Saxony-Anhalt

A deep porphyry quarry with excellent visibility.

## 9. Echinger See / Bavarian gravel lakes

Accessible, clear training lakes around Munich.

## 10. Nord-Ostsee-Kanal & coastal Baltic shore dives

Easy shore entries for cold-water experience and marine life.

## How diving in Germany works

Most inland sites are organised dive lakes with entry fees, fills, platforms,
and strict rules; many require local briefing or a guide and proof of
certification. This is cold, often low-visibility, drysuit-friendly diving —
exactly why German training standards are so respected.

## When to go

Lakes are diveable year-round; summer brings warmer surface water and longer
days, winter brings the clearest visibility (and ice-diving for the qualified).
The Baltic is best in calm summer windows. Thermoclines are sharp — expect cold
at depth any season.

## Safety and certification

Cold water raises the stakes: get drysuit training, dive within your
certification, follow site rules and buddy procedures, and respect depth and
deco discipline. German dive sites enforce rules strictly — that culture is a
feature.
