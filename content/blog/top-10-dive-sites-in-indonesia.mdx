Indonesia sits at the centre of the Coral Triangle and holds the richest
marine biodiversity on Earth. These ten dive areas are the global standouts.

## 1. Raja Ampat, West Papua

The most biodiverse marine region on the planet — walls, reefs, and fish
density that has to be seen to be believed.

## 2. Komodo National Park

Dramatic currents, mantas, and dragons above water — high-voltage diving.

## 3. Lembeh Strait, North Sulawesi

The muck-diving capital of the world — bizarre critters on black sand.

## 4. Bunaken, North Sulawesi

Sheer coral walls dropping into the blue, a classic marine park.

## 5. Wakatobi, Southeast Sulawesi

Pristine reefs and famous house-reef diving in a protected area.

## 6. Alor

Remote, current-fed reefs with extraordinary coral health.

## 7. Banda Sea (liveaboard)

Big-animal blue-water diving and hammerhead seasons.

## 8. Tulamben, Bali

The accessible USAT Liberty wreck — one of the world's best shore dives.

## 9. Nusa Penida, Bali

Mola mola (oceanic sunfish) and manta cleaning stations in cool currents.

## 10. Derawan & Sangalaki, Kalimantan

Turtles, mantas, and a jellyfish lake in the Celebes Sea.

## How diving in Indonesia works

Most premier sites are accessed by liveaboard (Raja Ampat, Komodo, Banda) or
dedicated dive resorts (Lembeh, Bunaken, Bali). Marine-park fees apply and fund
conservation. This is often advanced, current-driven diving — many sites
require proven experience, drift skills, and sometimes a minimum logged-dive
count.

## When to go

It is regional: Raja Ampat is best roughly October–April; Komodo's prime is
April–November; Bali's Mola season is around July–October; Lembeh is year-round.
Match the destination to its season — and check for monsoon and crossing-day
swell.

## Safety and conservation

Dive within your certification and experience (currents here are serious),
choose operators with strong safety and conservation records, maintain perfect
buoyancy, touch nothing, use reef-safe sun protection, and respect marine-park
rules. The Coral Triangle is fragile and globally irreplaceable.
